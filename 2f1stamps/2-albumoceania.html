Australia

Issue date: March 2007

A commemorative sheet celebrating the success of the Ferrari F1 team from 1996 to 2006, which is the period over which Michael Schumacher drove for Ferrari. During this time Ferrari won the constructors' title six times and Schumacher won the drivers' title five times.

Issue date: 2005

A souvenir sheet featuring the 10th anniversary of the Foster's Australian Grand Prix at Albert Park, Melbourne. The Australian Grand Prix was originally held at Adelaide, a street circuit considered to be one of greatest in the world. The race was held there from 1985 to 1995. From 1996 the race ws moved to Melbourne, this was a controversial move and a series of protests were organised by the "Save Albert Park" group, as it meant turning a public park into a private playground for one week each year. Approximately 401,000 people turned out for the first race at Albert Park. The Adelaide races were always the final race of each season; whereas the Albert Park races tend to be season openers, and so have seen many debuts.

Issue date: 27th February 2002

These stamps celebrate some of the great cars, drivers and events of Australian motor racing history. Including, Sir Jack Brabham, shown racing his Australian-engineered Repco-Brabham at Sandown Park in 1966. This car took Sir Jack to victory at the World Formula One Drivers' Championship and the World Formula One Constructors' Championship that same year. And in 1980, Alan Jones (Williams FW07 Ford) won both the Australian Grand Prix at Calder and the World Formula One Drivers' Championship. Australian Mark Weber was official reserve and test driver for the Renault-owned Benetton team in 2001. In his fifth year of international racing, Weber won three F3000 races as lead driver for Super Nova Racing and went on to claim second place in the 2001 F3000 International Championship.

Issue date: 31st July 2009

This sheet recognises Mark Webber securing his maiden Formula One victory and Australia's first victory since 1981. Not only is it the first F1 win for Australia in 28 years, but Mark Webber is only one of 3 Australian's to have ever won a grand prix - the others being Alan Jones and Jack Brabham. He won for the Red Bull F1 racing team at the German GP at the Nurburgring (on 12th July 2009) in his Red Bull Renault. The shots on the selvedge are of Mark Webber with the Grand Prix trophy, Mark Webber on the winners podium, Mark Webber leading the race and the Red Bull Renault car. The tabs show Mark Webber with and without helmet and the Red Bull Renault car.
New Zealand

Issue date: 4th February 2009

A set of stamps featuring various champions of motorsport. The set features Denis Hulme and Bruce McLaren. Denis Hulme raced in F1 from 1965 to 1974, he drove for Brabham for the first three years and then for McLaren for the rest of his time in F1. The 1967 Championship was essentially an internal affair within the Brabham team for most of the year, but the new Lotus 49 gave Jim Clark and Graham Hill the opportunity to bite back. But two wins in the 11-race Championship, at Monte Carlo and the ferocious Nurburgring (the Green Hell), and a series of strong points finishes, gave Hulme the advantage. He won the Championship by five points from Jack Brabham, and a further five from Jim Clark. Hulme was the first (and to date, only) F1 World Champion from New Zealand. In 1968 Hulme joined fellow Kiwi Bruce McLaren at his team; Hulme won a few races over the years at McLaren and managed third in the drivers championship in 1968 and 1972. Bruce McLaren raced in F1 from 1958 to 1970; he drove for Cooper before establishing his own team in 1966. His career best was a second and third in the drivers championship with Cooper and a third in the drivers championship with his own team. McLaren was a competitive driver but in many ways his legacy, the McLaren Racing Team, is testimony to his abilities as an analyst, engineer and manger that contributed much to the success of the cars that bore his name. Bruce McLaren died (aged 32) when his Can-Am car crashed on the Lavant Straight just before Woodcote corner at the Goodwood Circuit on 2nd June 1970.

Issue date: 2007

A mini sheet of values commemorating 60 years of Ferrari in F1. The Scuderia Ferrari was founded by Enzo Ferrari, initially to race cars produced by Alfa Romeo, though by 1947 Ferrari had begun building its own cars. The team was initially based in Modena from its pre-war founding until 1943, when Enzo Ferrari moved the team to a new factory in Maranello in 1943. As a constructor, Ferrari has a record 16 Constructors' Championships, the last of which was won in 2008. Alberto Ascari, Juan Manuel Fangio, Mike Hawthorn, Phil Hill, John Surtees, Niki Lauda, Jody Scheckter, Michael Schumacher and Kimi Raikkonen have won a record 15 Drivers' Championships for the team.[

Issue date: 2013

A mini sheet issued to commemorate Sebastian Vettel becoming the four times World F1 Driver Champion. Vettel won the World F1 Drivers' Championship in 2010, 2011, 2012 and 2013 with Red Bull Racing, making him one of the most successful F1 drivers of all time. He is currently the youngest driver ever to win the World Drivers' Championship and the youngest double, triple and quadruple world champion in F1. On October 4, 2014, Red Bull Racing announced that Vettel would be leaving the team at the end of the season. Team boss Christian Horner confirmed that Vettel announced his intention to trigger an exit clause in his contract to sign a deal with Ferrari for the 2015 season.
