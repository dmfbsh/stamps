Introduction

In 1950, as an answer to the Motorcycle World Championships introduced in 1949, the Federation Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA) organised the first ever official World Championship for drivers using the Formula One rules - the first official Formula One race was held at Silverstone on 13th May 1950. Motor racing actually started in France, as a direct result of the enthusiasm with which the French public embraced the motor car - the first ever motor race took place on 22nd July 1894.

Ever since the inception of the World Championship for drivers, the cars, drivers, teams and circuits have been featured on postage stamps from across the world. And since that first race in 1950, the sport has grown to become the most prestigous and expensive sport in the world.

This web site charts the history of Formula One using postage stamps.
